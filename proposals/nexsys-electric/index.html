03Scope of Work

The work is organized into four phases, delivered in sequence. Each builds on the last. These pieces work together as one system, so this proposal is built as a complete launch package rather than a pick-and-choose menu.

Phase 1, Google Business Profile Setup

  1. Claim & Verify the GBP Claim Nexsys Electric LLC on Google Business (business.google.com), complete verification (postcard or phone), and establish the profile as the authoritative digital anchor for the business.
  2. Fully Optimize the Profile Set primary category to Electrician, add secondary categories (electrical panel, commercial electrician), write a keyword-rich business description, upload photos, set service areas for Idaho Falls, Ammon, Pocatello, Blackfoot, Rexburg, Rigby, and Shelley, and add all services including panel upgrades, commercial electrical, and residential wiring.
  3. Connect & Configure Link the GBP to the new website once built, set up messaging, add Q&A content for common electrical questions, and verify business hours and contact info are accurate across the profile.

Phase 2, Website Build

A focused, fast, SEO-ready website structured to rank for the searches that generate electrical service calls in SE Idaho.

  1. Core Service Pages Dedicated pages for each primary service: Residential Electrical, Commercial Electrical, Electrical Panel Upgrades & Replacements, Wiring & Rewiring, EV Charger Installation, and Lighting. Each page is written to rank for its service type in the Idaho Falls market.
  2. City / Location Pages Individual pages for each key SE Idaho market: Idaho Falls, Ammon, Pocatello, Blackfoot, Rexburg, Rigby, and Shelley. Each page targets "[service] in [city]" search queries — for example, "electrician in Pocatello" or "panel upgrade in Rexburg" — and links back to relevant service pages so the whole site works together to help Nexsys show up for the searches customers actually make.
  3. Trust-Building Pages About page (Nexsys story, licensing, insurance, values), a contact page with embedded map and quote request form, and a reviews showcase page that pulls in and displays Google reviews to reinforce credibility.
  4. Technical Foundation Mobile-first design, fast load times (under 2 seconds on a phone), SSL/HTTPS security, structured data markup (site setup that helps Google understand who Nexsys is, what services they offer, and what areas they serve), Google Analytics and Search Console connected, sitemap submitted. The site is built to rank, not just look good.

Phase 3, Review Generation System

  1. Review Request Workflow A simple, repeatable post-job process: a direct Google review link sent via text message after every completed job. We write the message template and set up a shortlink so it takes 15 seconds per job to send.
  2. Target & Tracking Target: 50 reviews in 90 days if Nexsys consistently sends review requests after completed jobs. 6-month target: 150 reviews, which would put Nexsys ahead of every pure electrical competitor in SE Idaho. We set up a simple dashboard so the count is always visible. These are targets, not guarantees — results depend on consistent follow-through after each job.
  3. Response Templates Pre-written templates for responding to positive and negative reviews in the Nexsys voice, professional, warm, and specific. Responding to reviews signals quality and care to both Google's algorithm and prospective customers reading them.

Phase 4, Google Local Services Ads (LSA)

  1. Verification & Setup Complete the Google Guaranteed verification process: license submission, insurance verification, and background check. This unlocks the LSA badge, which displays above all standard search results and organic listings.
  2. Campaign Configuration Set up the LSA campaign for Idaho Falls and the surrounding SE Idaho service area. Configure service types (panels, residential, commercial), budget, and lead type preferences. LSAs charge per verified lead, not per click, the most efficient paid channel for local service businesses.
  3. Ongoing Optimization Monthly review of lead quality, disputed charge processing, and bid adjustments. As review count grows, LSA rankings improve automatically, the system rewards reputation.

Ongoing, Local SEO & Presence Maintenance

A one-time build fades. Monthly maintenance keeps the presence compounding:

  • GBP posts (2 per month), job spotlights, seasonal tips, panel upgrade reminders
  • Review monitoring and response within 48 hours
  • Monthly search ranking report for core keywords
  • Quarterly site content refresh, new city pages, updated service descriptions, blog posts targeting SE Idaho electrical questions
  • Directory listing cleanup — making sure the business name, address, and phone number match correctly across important online listings like Yelp, Angi, and local directories
Important Local SEO is not a one-time project. The businesses that show up consistently in local search maintain consistent activity — reviews, content, Google Business Profile updates — over time. The monthly plan is what makes the initial setup investment compound rather than fade. Monthly growth services require a 3-month initial commitment after launch. Local SEO, reviews, and Google visibility need consistent activity to build momentum.

Phase 5, Social Media Presence

A website and Google profile get Nexsys found when someone is already searching. Social media keeps the name visible in between, in the local feeds, neighborhood groups, and home improvement conversations where work gets recommended before anyone opens Google. For a new business, that ambient visibility builds the trust that converts a cold search into a call.

  1. Profile Setup & Optimization Optimize the existing Facebook Business page (categories, services, cover image, contact info, booking link). Create and connect an Instagram Business profile. Set up a verified Nextdoor Business profile, the highest-trust platform for local service referrals, where neighbors actively ask "who's a good electrician near me?"
  2. Branded Content Templates Build a reusable set of on-brand post templates in the Nexsys visual style, job spotlights, tips, service announcements, and review highlights. Consistent look across all three platforms from day one.
  3. Monthly Content & Management 10 posts per month on Facebook and Instagram, before/after job photos, electrical tips, local presence posts, seasonal content, and review shoutouts. 2 posts per month on Nextdoor. Captions written in plain language the owner would actually use. Comment and DM monitoring on weekdays.
  4. Monthly Reporting A short monthly summary: reach, follows, engagement, and any notable interactions. No jargon, just what's working and what the next month's content will focus on.
